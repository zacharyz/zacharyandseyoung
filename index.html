The Wedding

‹ ›

Seyoung and Zachary are getting married on September 14, 2013, after seven years of being a couple. They met and began dating in Eugene, Oregon and have continued to build their life together in Portland, Oregon with their beloved cat Bella. The couple enjoy enriching their relationship with shared experiences like traveling, exploring local food and entertainment offered in Portland, and exchanging silly inside jokes.

There is a Korean folktale about a poor but kind man who saved a barn swallow's life. The barn swallow repaid his kindness with a gourd seed. The man planted the seed and grew several giant gourds. When the man and his family cut them open, the gourds were filled with riches that helped the family live comfortably and happily for the rest of their days.

We have adopted the value of kindness begetting happiness to become the theme for our wedding. In the years that we have been together many have shown support for the solidity of our relationship. The theme of this wedding is dedicated to these people, whose support and kindness have helped us to pursue our happiness, as the barn swallows did in the story.

We would like to invite you to celebrate with us the seven years passed and the many years that will follow, and allow us to thank you for your support throughout the years.

Directions

The Wedding and Reception will be held at the Historic Overlook House in north Portland.

Northbound from Interstate 5:

  • Take Exit 303 (Swan Island) Stay left to Interstate Ave.
  • Turn left and proceed 3/10 miles on Interstate Ave.
  • Turn right on N. Overlook Blvd.
  • Proceed two blocks.
  • Veer Left onto Melrose Drive (one way) and proceed 2/10 mile.
  • The Overlook House is located on the left

Southbound from Interstate 5:

  • Take Exit 303 (Alberta Street).
  • At first stop sign (Alberta Street) turn right.
  • At first light (Interstate Avenue) turn left and proceed 1/2 miles.
  • Turn right on N. Overlook Blvd.
  • Proceed two blocks.
  • Veer Left onto Melrose Drive (one way) proceed 2/10 mile.
  • The Overlook House is located on the left.

TriMet's Max Yellow Line (Portland's Light Rail System):

The Interstate Max/Yellow line "Overlook Park" Station is three blocks from the Overlook House. From the station (at the corner of Interstate Ave and Overlook Blvd) walk west on Overlook Blvd., along the park proceeding left for 2/10 of a mile on Melrose Drive (one way) Overlook House is located on the left.

Portland

We have called Portland our home since 2010. Even though we have traveled all over the world together we still look forward to returning home here.

We think Portland is pretty awesome, and hope you will think so to. To help you along in your journey of discovery we have created a list of our favorite places to see and thinkgs do while in Portland. So even after the festivities have ended you will hopefully have something awesome to do.

Portland is quickly becoming known as one of the best places in the world for beer (considered the #1 beer city in the US), coffee, and food.

Finding Portland from Uncage the Soul Productions on Vimeo.

Stuff to eat

  • Tasty n Tasty - some of the best brunch in town.
  • Salt and Straw - probably the best ice cream ever.
  • Pok Pok - Thai food from a James Beard award winning chef. Try the world famous fish sauce wings.
  • Por Que No - Portland's take on Mexican food. Try the Bryan's Bowl.
  • Ken's Artisan Pizza - amazing pizza.
  • Nicholas Restaurant - great Lebanese and Middle Eastern Cuisine.
  • Pine State Biscuits - simple but oh so great Biscuits and Gravy - the line is worth it.
  • Lardo - Sandwiches designed to make you fat.
  • Bunk Bar - More great Sandwiches - try the pork belly cubano.
  • Blossuming Lotus - The best vegan food in town - try the live tacos.
  • Koi Fusion - Korean/Mexican fusion (food cart).
  • Potato Champion - Portland's take on Pountine (food cart).
  • Nong's Khao Man Gai - Portland's most famous food cart (and our caterer) serving Thai style chicken and rice.
  • Euro Trash - Portuguese/Euro styling cooking (food cart).
  • Waffle Window - awesome and cheap waffles with delicious local ingredients.
  • Gaufre Gourmet - another take on waffles (food cart).
  • Hungry Heart Cupcakes - Portland's only completely mobile cupcakery (food cart).

Stuff to drink

  • Barista - exceptional coffee from the best baristas
  • Stumptown - Portland's most famous coffee roaster.
  • Water Avenue Coffee Company - another local favorite.
  • Bailey's Taproom - A selection of amazing local brews
  • Hopworks - local brewery.
  • Deschutes Brewery - another oregon brewery.
  • Portland City Grill - Enjoy their Happy Hour and the spectacular view of the city from the highest vantage point you can access within the city.
  • Departure - Also great for the view from the higher vantages of Portlandâ€™s urbanscape.

Stuff to do

  • Washington Park - Japanese Gardens, Rose Gardens and an awesome view.
  • Rocky Butte - another great view.
  • Multnomah Falls and the Gorge - a waterfall wonderland with lots of river activities, wine country, fruit and floral u-picks, hiking and stunning viewpoints.
  • Powell's Books
  • Sauvie Island - Right by the heart of Portland is an island full of farm country, fruit and floral u-picks, and beaches (yes, even nudie ones).
